- **Sūrya‑Siddhānta** – an astronomical siddhānta (post‑5th c. CE), **not** sage‑attributed in your sense【turn52search0】【turn52search7】  
- **Bhāgavata Purāṇa** (Śrīmad‑Bhāgavatam) – a Purāṇa, not a Vedic/Sūtra text【turn50fetch0】【turn51fetch0】  

I have **not** found a Vedic Saṃhitā‑Brāhmaṇa‑Āraṇyaka or an early Sūtra/Smṛti that actually defines *paramāṇu* or *truṭi* as a **time unit**. So, under your criteria, **I cannot honestly give a paramāṇu‑based chain from strictly sage‑attributed texts**.

## 2. Sage‑attributed scheme (up to 1 year)

### 2.1 Core textual sources

The following **sage‑attributed** texts explicitly define time units:

1. **Manusmṛti (Smṛti, traditionally authored by Svāyambhuva Manu via Bṛgu)**  
   - **1.64–66** defines: *nimeṣa, kāṣṭhā, kalā, muhūrta, ahorātra, pakṣa, māsa, ayana, saṃvatsara*【turn12fetch0】【turn7fetch0】.

2. **Suśruta Saṃhitā (medical Saṃhitā; traditionally taught by Dhanvantari, an avatāra, to Suśruta)**  
   - **Sūtrasthāna 6/4** (VI.4): defines *akṣi‑nimeṣa, kāṣṭhā, muhūrta, ahorātra, pakṣa, māsa, ṛtu, ayana, saṃvatsara*【turn31fetch0】.

3. **Caraka Saṃhitā (medical Saṃhitā; traditionally by Agniveśa, revised by Caraka)**  
   - **Sūtrasthāna 4/5** (section “Division of time in classical literature”) gives a full table:  
     *akṣi‑nimeṣa (mātra), kāṣṭhā, kalā, nāḍikā, muhūrta, yāma, ahorātra, pakṣa, māsa, ṛtu, ayana, varṣa*【turn34fetch0】.

4. **Taittirīya Āraṇyaka (Vedic/Āraṇyaka, part of Kṛṣṇa Yajurveda)**  
   - **10.1.2** is cited as stating that from the “vidyut‑puruṣa” arise *nimeṣa, kalā, muhūrta, kāṣṭhā, ahorātra* etc., as components of a *saṃvatsara*【turn28fetch0】.

All of these are **sage‑attributed** in your sense (Vedic ṛṣi or avatāra‑related), and they share essentially the same **human‑scale** time units.

### 2.2 Chain of units from the smallest *defined* unit up to 1 year

Because Manu and Suśruta only define down to **nimeṣa/kāṣṭhā**, while Caraka also gives a finer subdivision starting from **akṣi‑nimeṣa**, I will give **two variant chains**:

- **Chain A – Manusmṛti & Suśruta (down to nimeṣa/kāṣṭhā)**  
- **Chain B – Caraka (starts from akṣi‑nimeṣa)**

You can treat Chain B as the **more detailed but still sage‑attributed** version.

#### 2.2.1 Chain A – Manusmṛti & Suśruta (minimal)

From **Manusmṛti 1.64–66**【turn12fetch0】 and **Suśruta Saṃhitā Sū. 6/4**【turn31fetch0】:

1. **Nimeṣa (निमेष)** – “blink of the eye”  
   - Manu: defined as the time of one wink or of pronouncing one syllable【turn7fetch0】.  
   - Suśruta: *akṣi‑nimeṣa* is the time taken to articulate a short vowel【turn31fetch0】.

2. **Kāṣṭhā (काष्ठा)**  
   - **Suśruta**: “Fifteen *akṣi‑nimeṣas* make one *kāṣṭhā*.”【turn31fetch0】  
   - Manu’s verse is read by some commentators as **18 nimeṣas = 1 kāṣṭhā**, but the printed text and most summaries follow **15 nimeṣas = 1 kāṣṭhā**【turn7fetch0】【turn31fetch0】.

3. **Kalā (कला)**  
   - **30 kāṣṭhās = 1 kalā**【turn7fetch0】【turn31fetch0】.

4. **Muhūrta (मुहूर्त)**  
   - **30 kalās = 1 muhūrta**【turn7fetch0】【turn31fetch0】.

5. **Ahorātra (अहोरात्र – day + night)**  
   - **30 muhūrtas = 1 ahorātra**【turn7fetch0】【turn31fetch0】.

6. **Pakṣa (पक्ष – fortnight)**  
   - **15 ahorātras = 1 pakṣa**【turn31fetch0】.

7. **Māsa (मास – month)**  
   - **2 pakṣas = 1 māsa**【turn31fetch0】.

8. **Ṛtu (ऋतु – season)**  
   - **2 māsas = 1 ṛtu**【turn31fetch0】.

9. **Ayana (अयन – half‑year)**  
   - **3 ṛtus = 1 ayana**【turn12fetch0】.

10. **Saṃvatsara / Varṣa (संवत्सर / वर्ष – year)**  
    - **2 ayanas = 1 saṃvatsara / varṣa**【turn12fetch0】【turn31fetch0】.

This already satisfies your request: **a continuous chain from a defined small unit up to one year**, using only sage‑attributed texts.

#### 2.2.2 Chain B – Caraka Saṃhitā (finer, still sage‑attributed)

**Caraka Saṃhitā, Sūtrasthāna 4/5** gives a finer subdivision, starting from **akṣi‑nimeṣa (mātra)**【turn34fetch0】:

1. **Akṣi‑nimeṣa / Mātra (अक्षिनिमेष / मात्रा)**  
   - Time taken in blinking the eyelids; identified as the **smallest unit** in this system【turn34fetch0】.

2. **Kāṣṭhā (काष्ठा)**  
   - **15 mātras = 1 kāṣṭhā**【turn34fetch0】.

3. **Kalā (कला)**  
   - **30 kāṣṭhās = 1 kalā**【turn34fetch0】.

4. **Nāḍikā (नाडिका)**  
   - **20 + 1/10 kalās = 1 nāḍikā**【turn34fetch0】.

5. **Muhūrta (मुहूर्त)**  
   - **2 nāḍikās = 1 muhūrta**【turn34fetch0】.

6. **Yāma (याम – “watch”, 1/8 of day or night)**  
   - **3.75 muhūrtas = 1 yāma**【turn34fetch0】.

7. **Ahorātra (अहोरात्र)**  
   - **4 yāmas = 1 day; 4 yāmas = 1 night** → 8 yāmas = 1 ahorātra【turn34fetch0】.

8. **Pakṣa (पक्ष)**  
   - **15 ahorātras = 1 pakṣa**【turn34fetch0】.

9. **Māsa (मास)**  
   - **2 pakṣas = 1 māsa**【turn34fetch0】.

10. **Ṛtu (ऋतु)**  
    - **2 māsas = 1 ṛtu**【turn34fetch0】.

11. **Ayana (अयन)**

12. **Varṣa (वर्ष – year)**

This is **fully sage‑attributed** and gives a **finer starting unit** than Manu/Suśruta.

## 3. Why I am **not** including a paramāṇu‑based chain under “sage‑attributed”

You specifically asked for:

> from the smallest unit (I think it might be paramāṇu) to 1 year, based on critical classical sage‑attributed texts.

Based on my searches:

- The **atomic‑level time units** – *paramāṇu, aṇu, trasareṇu, truṭi, vedha, lava* etc. – are **clearly defined** in:
  - **Sūrya‑Siddhānta**, an astronomical siddhānta (historical, not sage‑attributed in your sense)【turn52search0】【turn52search7】.
  - **Śrīmad‑Bhāgavatam (Bhāgavata Purāṇa) 3.11**, where *paramāṇu* is the ultimate particle, and *truṭi* is defined as the time for the sun to cross three *trasareṇus*【turn50fetch0】【turn51fetch0】.

So, under your strict definition of **sage‑attributed = Vedic ṛṣi‑related Śruti or authoritative Smṛti**:

- **Paramāṇu‑based time measurement is *not* attested in those layers.**
- The **earliest clearly sage‑attributed metrology** starts with **nimeṣa / akṣi‑nimeṣa**, not with *paramāṇu*.

That is why the lists above stop at **akṣi‑nimeṣa / mātra** as the smallest sage‑attested unit.
