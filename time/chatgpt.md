# Hindu Units of Time (Arṣa/Sage-Attributed Sources)

Early **Vedic–Purāṇic** texts (Śruti and Arṣa-Smṛti) give a detailed chronology from sub-second to one year (360 days).  The *Bhāgavata Purāṇa* (3.11) and related Arṣa works enumerate the chain of units: 

- **परमाणु (paramāṇu)** – smallest time unit (time for the sun’s ray to traverse a *paramāṇu*, the finest particle)【50†L291-L293】.  
- **अणु (āṇu)** – two *paramāṇu* (an *āṇu* is thus 2 paramāṇu)【50†L291-L293】.  
- **त्रसरेणु (trasareṇu)** – three *āṇu* (visible in sunlit dust)【50†L291-L293】.  
- **त्रुटि (truṭi)** – time to cross three *trasareṇu*; one *truṭi* ≈ 0.000474 s【50†L307-L310】.  
- **व॒ध (vedha)** – 100 *truṭi*【50†L307-L310】.  
- **लव (lava)** – 3 *vedha* (≈0.14 s)【50†L307-L310】.  
- **निमेष (niméṣa)** – 3 *lava* (~0.43 s)【50†L323-L329】.  
- **क्षण (kṣaṇa)** – 3 *niméṣa* (~1.28 s)【50†L323-L329】.  
- **काष्ठा (kāṣṭhā)** – 5 *kṣaṇa* (≈6.4 s)【50†L323-L329】.  
- **लघु (laghu)** – 15 *kāṣṭhā* (≈96 s)【50†L323-L329】.  

For example, Bhāgavata 3.11.5–7 states: “Two *paramāṇu* make one *āṇu*, three *āṇu* make one *trasareṇu*…” and “three *trasareṇu* = one *truṭi*; one hundred *truṭi* = one *vedha*; three *vedha* = one *lava*; three *lava* = one *niméṣa*; three *niméṣa* = one *kṣaṇa*; five *kṣaṇa* = one *kāṣṭhā*; fifteen *kāṣṭhā* = one *laghu*”【50†L291-L293】【50†L307-L310】. 

- **नाडीका / दण्ड (nāḍikā/danḍa)** – 15 *laghu* (≈24 min)【50†L339-L340】. Two spellings (*nāḍikā* or *daṇḍa*) appear in classical texts for a 24-minute unit.  
- **मुहूर्त (muhūrta)** – 2 *nāḍikā* (≈48 min)【50†L339-L340】.  
- **प्रहर / याम (praḥāra / yāma)** – 6 (or sometimes 7) *nāḍikā* (~3–3.5 h), i.e. one quarter of day (a “prahar” or “yāmā”)【50†L339-L340】.  

Bhāgavata 3.11.8–10 similarly gives: “Fifteen *laghu* = one *nāḍikā*; two *nāḍikā* = one *muhūrta*; six (ṣaṭ) or seven *nāḍikā* = one *prahar* (yāma)”【50†L339-L340】.  (This agrees with later sources: 15 × 96 s ≈ 1440 s = 24 min per *nāḍikā*; 2×24 min = 48 min per *muhūrta*【10†L890-L894】; 6–7×24 min ≈ 144–168 min per *prahar*.)  

- **अहोऱात्रम् (ahorātram)** – full day-night (24h = 30 *muhūrta*).  Four *prahar* (yāma) constitute one day or night【50†L377-L380】.  
- **पक्ष (pakṣa)** – fortnight (15 days).  Bhāgavata 3.11.10 notes “**पक्षः पञ्चदशाहानि**” (15 day-nights = one *pakṣa*)【50†L377-L380】.  
- **मास (māsa)** – month (30 days, two *pakṣa*).  This follows from the above (*dve pakṣa = 1 māsa*【36†L223-L225】).  
- **ऋतु (ṛtu)** – season (2 months).  (Not explicitly asked up to 1 year, but often given as 2 *māsa*.)  
- **आयन (āyana)** – half-year (6 *māsa*).  (Dakṣiṇāyana/Uttarāyana); two *āyana* = one full year【36†L223-L225】.  
- **संवत्सर (saṃvatsara)** – civil year (360 days).  By tradition a *saṃvatsara* is 12 months of 30 days each (2 *āyana*)【36†L223-L225】.  

Thus the sequence from smallest to year is:

- परमाणु *paramāṇu* (smallest unit)  
- अणु *āṇu* (2 paramāṇu)  
- त्रसरेणु *trasareṇu* (3 āṇu)  
- त्रुटि *truṭi* (3 trasareṇu)  
- व॒ध *vedha* (100 truṭi)  
- लव *lava* (3 vedha)  
- निमेष *niméṣa* (3 lava)  
- क्षण *kṣaṇa* (3 niméṣa)  
- काष्ठा *kāṣṭhā* (5 kṣaṇa)  
- लघु *laghu* (15 kāṣṭhā)  
- नाडीका/दण्ड *nāḍikā/danḍa* (15 laghu)  
- मुहूर्त *muhūrta* (2 nāḍikā)  
- प्रहर *prahrā/prāhara* (6–7 nāḍikā)  
- अहोरात्रम् *ahorātram* (day-night = 30 muhūrta)  
- पक्ष *pakṣa* (15 days)【50†L377-L380】  
- मास *māsa* (2 pakṣa = 30 days)【36†L223-L225】  
- ऋतु *ṛtu* (2 māsa)  
- आयन *āyana* (6 māsa)【36†L223-L225】  
- संवत्सर *saṃvatsara* (360 days = 12 māsa)【36†L223-L225】  

Each of these names and definitions is found in Arṣa scriptures.  For instance, Bhāgavata Purāṇa 3.11.5–8 explicitly lists the micro-units from *paramāṇu* up to *laghu*【50†L291-L293】【50†L323-L329】, and verse 8 gives *nāḍikā*, *muhūrta*, *prahar*【50†L339-L340】.  Other texts (e.g. Sūrya-siddhānta, Vāyu and Bhagavata Purāṇas) similarly define fortnight, month, half-year and year【36†L223-L225】【50†L377-L380】. 

**Sources:** Sanskrit verses from the Bhāgavata Purāṇa (3.11) for units up to laghu【50†L291-L293】【50†L323-L329】, and traditional Smṛti/Purāṇa references for larger units【50†L339-L340】【36†L223-L225】. These are Arṣa (rishi-attributed) texts.
