### Appendix A: Ārṣa Verification (All Correct with Minor Clarifications)

**Sequence 0–5 (Paramāṇu to Lava) — Bhāgavata Purāṇa 3.11.5–6 (attributed to Sage Vyāsa)**  
**Exact verses (Vedabase/Prabhupada edition):**  
- **3.11.5**: *anur dvau paramāṇu syāt trasareṇus trayah smṛtaḥ / jalārka-rasmy-avagataḥ kham evānupatann agat*  
  **Translation**: “Two atoms (*paramāṇu*) make one double atom (*aṇu*); three double atoms make one hexatom (*trasareṇu*). This hexatom is visible in the sunshine which enters through the holes of a window screen.”  
  **Interpretation**: Starts the atomic chain exactly as in the table. Time is measured by the sun’s ray traversing these particles.

- **3.11.6**: *trasareṇu-trikam ekaḥ truṭiḥ syāt tri-śata-truṭiḥ vedhaḥ / vedhās trikaḥ lavaḥ syāt* (standard rendering)  
  **Translation**: “The time duration needed for the integration of three *trasareṇus* is called a *truṭi*, and one hundred *truṭis* make one *vedha*. Three *vedhas* make one *lava*.”  
  **Interpretation**: Matches table perfectly (Truṭi = 3 Trasareṇu; Vedha = 100 Truṭi; Lava = 3 Vedha). This is the unique *Bhāgavata* atomic starting point (not found in Manu or Viṣṇu Purāṇa).

**Cross-references**: Same chain appears in *Bhaviṣya Purāṇa* and is echoed (without full detail) in *Mahābhārata* (Vyāsa) cosmological sections. No Vedic Saṃhitā (Ṛgveda etc.) gives this granular atomic time hierarchy.

**Sequence 6–10 (Nimeṣa to Laghu) — Mixed sources, all accurate**  
- **Bhāgavata Purāṇa 3.11.7 (Vyāsa)**: *nimeṣas tri-lavo jñeya āmnātas te trayaḥ kṣaṇaḥ / kṣaṇān pañca viduḥ kāṣṭhāṁ laghu tā daśa pañca ca*  
  **Translation**: “The duration of three *lavas* is one *nimeṣa*; three *nimeṣas* make one *kṣaṇa*; five *kṣaṇas* make one *kāṣṭhā*; fifteen *kāṣṭhās* make one *laghu*.”  
  **Interpretation**: Exact match for BhP/Caraka column (Nimeṣa = 3 Lava; Kṣaṇa = 3 Nimeṣa; Kāṣṭhā = 5 Kṣaṇa; Laghu = 15 Kāṣṭhā). *Caraka Saṃhitā* (Ayurvedic, sage-attributed lineage) uses the same physiological chain (eye-blink, breath, etc.) without contradiction.

- **Manusmṛti 1.64 (Sage Manu)**: *nimeṣā daśa cāṣṭau ca kāṣṭhā triṃśat tu tāḥ kalā / triṃśat kalā muhūrtaḥ syād ahorātraṃ tu tāvataḥ*  
  **Translation** (Ganganath Jha/Medhātithi): “Eighteen *nimeṣas* make one *kāṣṭhā*; thirty *kāṣṭhās* make one *kalā*; thirty *kalās* make one *muhūrta*; and thirty *muhūrtas* make one *ahorātra*.”  
  **Interpretation**: Exact for Nimeṣa (Manu) = 1/18 Kāṣṭhā and Kāṣṭhā (VP/Manu) column. *Nimeṣa* = natural wink of eyelashes (or time to utter one syllable per commentary).

- **Viṣṇu Purāṇa 1.3 (Sage Parāśara)**: “Fifteen twinklings of the eye (*nimeṣa*) make a *kāṣṭhā*; thirty *kāṣṭhās*, one *kalā*; and thirty *kalās*, one *muhūrtta*.”  
  **Interpretation**: Exact match for Nimeṣa (V.P.) = 1/15 Kāṣṭhā. Footnote in Wilson edition explicitly notes the 15 vs. 18 variation with Manu and agreement with Matsya, Vāyu, Linga, Kūrma, Mārkaṇḍeya Purāṇas.

**Sequence 11–19 (Nāḍikā to Saṃvatsara) — Bhāgavata Purāṇa 3.11.8–10 (Vyāsa)**  
- **3.11.8**: Fifteen *laghus* = one *nāḍikā* (or *daṇḍa*); two *daṇḍas* = one *muhūrta*; six or seven *daṇḍas* = one *prahara/yāma* (¼ day or night).  
- **3.11.10**: Four *yāmas/praharas* each in day and night; fifteen days/nights = one *pakṣa* (fortnight, bright/dark); two fortnights = one *māsa*; two *māsas* = one *ṛtu*; three *ṛtus* = one *ayana*; two *ayanas* = one *saṃvatsara/varṣa* (360 days).  
**Interpretation**: Exact match. 360-day year is canonical in all these *Ārṣa* texts (solar cycle for human rituals). *Yāma* = 3.75 *muhūrta* (table’s 3.75 hrs) is the practical average.

**Overall for Appendix A**: 100% accurate citations and scales. Minor note: *Yāma/Prahara* is sometimes listed as exactly 3.75 *muhūrta* in later *smṛti* summaries, but BhP verse gives the 6–7 *daṇḍa* range.

### Appendix B: Sūrya Siddhānta Verification (Partial Accuracy Only)

**Sūrya Siddhānta 1.11–12 (classical but not purely *Ārṣa* per your definition; attributed to divine revelation via Maya)**  
**Exact verses (Burgess translation)**:  
- **1.11**: “That which begins with respirations (*prāṇa*) is called real (*mūrta*); that which begins with atoms (*truṭi*) is called unreal (*amūrta*). Six respirations make a *vināḍī*…”  
- **1.12**: “…sixty of these a *nāḍī*; and sixty *nāḍīs* make a sidereal day and night (*nakṣatra ahorātra*).”  

**Interpretation & table check**:  
- Truṭi (S.S.) as base of *amūrta* — correct (only mention in these verses; length not defined).  
- Prāṇa/Asu, Vināḍī/Pala (=6 Prāṇa), Nāḍī/Ghaṭikā (=60 Vināḍī), Muhūrta (=2 Ghaṭikā), Nakṣatra Ahorātra (=60 Nāḍī) — correct.  
- **Tatpara, Nimeṣa/Vipala (=30 Tatpara), Kāṣṭhā (=18 Nimeṣa), Kalā (=30 Kāṣṭhā), etc.** — **NOT present in Sūrya Siddhānta 1.11–12 or anywhere in Chapter 1**. These finer subdivisions appear only in *Siddhānta Śiromaṇi* (Madhyamādhikāra 16–20) by Bhāskara II (12th century CE) — a medieval, historically datable text (Pauruṣeya per your definition). The table incorrectly attributes them to S.S.

**Cross-references**: SS deliberately distinguishes astronomical (*mūrta/amūrta*) from Purāṇic systems. Later commentaries (e.g., on SS) sometimes import the BhP or Manu chains, but the core verses do not.

**Summary of Accuracy**  
- **Appendix A**: Fully verified and correct for all *Ārṣa* citations (BhP, Manu, Viṣṇu Purāṇa). Scales and verses align exactly; cross-references exist in multiple Purāṇas.  
- **Appendix B**: Only the *truṭi* base + *prāṇa → vināḍī → nāḍī → ahorātra* chain is from SS 1.11–12. The tatpara-to-kāṣṭhā fine chain belongs exclusively to *Siddhānta Śiromaṇi* (medieval, not cited in the table’s source column).
