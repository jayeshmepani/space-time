# 1) **VAISHNAVISM**

## **Supreme Reality: Vishnu / Narayana / Mahavishnu**

In **Vaishnavism**, the Supreme is **Vishnu** (or in many schools, **Narayana**, and in others **Krishna as Svayam Bhagavan**).
He is the **preserver, sustainer, indwelling Lord, cosmic source**, and the one through whom creation is ordered and maintained.

In the theological model **you specifically asked for**, the most important triad is the **Three Purusha forms of Vishnu**:

---

## 🔷 **Highest Hierarchy in Vaishnavism**

### **1. Mahavishnu (Karanodakashayi Vishnu)**

### **2. Garbhodakashayi Vishnu**

### **3. Kshirodakashayi Vishnu (Paramatma)**

This threefold structure is standard in several Vaishnava traditions, especially **Gaudiya Vaishnavism**, where these are described as distinct cosmic manifestations of the Lord. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

## 🥇 **1. Mahavishnu (Karanodakashayi Vishnu)**

### **Highest cosmic form**

This is the **supreme causal Vishnu** associated with the **Causal Ocean (Karana-jala / Karana Ocean)**.

### **Role**

* Source of innumerable universes
* The universes emerge from Him
* The first Purusha expansion into cosmic causality

### **Abode**

* **Causal Ocean**
* In many theological presentations: beyond or linked to **Vaikuntha**
* In Krishna-centered Vaishnavism, Mahavishnu is below the highest personal reality of Krishna, but within your requested structure he is the **top Purusha Vishnu**. ([Wikipedia][1])

### **Consort / Shakti**

* Ultimately linked to **Lakshmi**
* In some schools, Lakshmi expands with Narayana and Vishnu in multiple forms

### **Iconography**

* Reclining on the causal waters
* Deep blue complexion
* Four-armed
* Holds:

  * **Shankha** (conch)
  * **Chakra** (discus)
  * **Gada** (mace)
  * **Padma** (lotus)

### **Symbolism**

Mahavishnu is the **transcendent cosmic source** — not merely “a god in the universe,” but the **source of universes**.

---

## 🥈 **2. Garbhodakashayi Vishnu**

### **Cosmic expansion within each universe**

This is Vishnu entering **each individual universe**.

### **Role**

* Resides in each cosmic egg / universe
* From His navel emerges **Brahma**
* Serves as the source of internal cosmic creation

### **Abode**

* **Garbha Ocean** inside each universe

### **Consort / Shakti**

* Still the Vishnu principle with Lakshmi inseparably present in theological terms, though not always iconographically emphasized here

### **Iconography**

* Reclining on **Ananta / Shesha**
* Lotus arises from His navel
* Brahma seated on the lotus

### **Meaning**

If Mahavishnu is the **macrocosmic source**, Garbhodakashayi Vishnu is the **architectural entry into each cosmos**. ([Wikipedia][1])

---

## 🥉 **3. Kshirodakashayi Vishnu**

### **Immanent Vishnu / Paramatma**

This is the **most intimate, indwelling form**.

### **Role**

* Resides in the **Ocean of Milk**
* Present in the heart of all beings as **Paramatma**
* Oversees dharma and avatāric descent
* The form through which Vishnu interacts most directly with the universe

### **Abode**

* **Kshira Sagara / Milk Ocean**
* Sometimes associated with **Shvetadvipa** in Vaishnava theology ([Bhaktivedanta Ashram][2])

### **Consort**

* **Lakshmi**

### **Iconography**

* Reclining on serpent bed
* Attended by Lakshmi
* White ocean setting
* More relational and devotional in practice

### **Meaning**

This is the Vishnu who is:

* in the cosmos,
* in the heart,
* and in devotional accessibility.

The five faces correspond to:

* **Sadyojata**
* **Vamadeva**
* **Aghora**
* **Tatpurusha**
* **Ishana**

### **Meaning**

If Paramaśiva is **absolute transcendence**, then Sadāśiva is **transcendence becoming cosmically self-aware**.
