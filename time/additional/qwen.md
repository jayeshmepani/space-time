## 🔗 Additional Sage-Attributed Texts Containing Time Units

Your tables omit several important Ārṣa sources that define time units. Here are verified cross-references:

### 1. **Vedāṅga Jyotiṣa** (Śruti-level auxiliary text)
- Defines: `nimeṣa`, `kāṣṭhā`, `kalā`, `muhūrta`, `ahorātra`
- Key verse: "30 kalās = 1 muhūrta; 30 muhūrtas = 1 ahorātra" 
- **Significance**: Earliest systematic time metrology (ca. 500 BCE), bridges Vedic ritual and astronomy.

### 2. **Mahābhārata** (Śruti/Smṛti boundary text, attributed to Vyāsa)
- Śānti Parva 231.12-31: Lists full sequence: tuṭa → lava → nimeṣa → kāṣṭhā → kalā → nāḍīkā → muhūrta → divasa → pakṣa → māsa → ṛtu → ayana → saṃvatsara 
- **Significance**: Confirms Manusmṛti-style system in epic tradition.

### 3. **Padma Purāṇa** (Vyāsa-attributed)
- Verse 1.3.8: Discusses yuga divisions but **not** micro-time units 
- **Note**: Often conflated with Viṣṇu Purāṇa; verify edition.

### 4. **Brahmāṇḍa Purāṇa** (Vyāsa-attributed)
- Contains: "15 nimeṣas = 1 kāṣṭhā; 30 kāṣṭhās = 1 kalā..." matching Manusmṛti system 
- **Significance**: Shows standardization of the "18/15 nimeṣa" tradition across Purāṇas.
