**Om Śrī Gurubhyo Namaḥ. Om Namo Nārāyaṇāya.**

O blessed seeker of the eternal Vidyā, I am the Maha-Acharya, custodian of the Chaturdasha-Vidya, knower of the convergence of Siddhantic Jyotiṣa and the Shastric vision of the Brahmanda as revealed through the Ārṣa streams—from the Mantra-Ramayana echoed in the Rig Veda, through the Sthapatyaveda of cosmic geometry, to the full expanse of the Mukhya Puranas (headed by Śrīmad Bhāgavatam, Fifth Canto), the Upa- and Ati-Puranas, and the twenty-seven Sage-attributed streams of the Ramayana corpus: the Ādi of Vālmīki, the Adhyātma, Ānanda, Adbhuta, the non-dual Yogavāsiṣṭha, the Agastya, Lomaśa, Bhuśuṇḍi, the foundational Brahma-Ramayana of one billion ślokas, and the solar-lunar alignments of the Saurya and Chandra Ramayanas, as well as the Tattvic revelations of the Sūrya, Brahma, and Vasiṣṭha Siddhantas and the Vaishnava-Śaiva Āgamas.

In this Kali-Kalpa, as in every Kalpa, the structure of the Brahmanda remains the eternal blueprint of the sages, with only the subtlest variations in emphasis—philosophical in the Ramayanas, astronomical in the Siddhantas—yet one in essence. I shall now unfold the multi-dimensional map of the Brahmanda exactly as the Rishis have seen it in samādhi, without the taint of later speculation.

### 1. The Origin & The Oceans: Manifestation from the Causal Ocean

From the boundless Karana Samudra—the Causal Ocean wherein lies Mahā-Viṣṇu (Kāraṇodakaśāyī Viṣṇu), the first Puruṣa-avatāra—issues the glance of the Lord. By that divine glance the mahat-tattva is agitated, and innumerable Brahmandas manifest like bubbles in the cosmic waters. Each Brahmanda is a self-contained Cosmic Egg.

Within each Brahmanda reposes Garbhodakaśāyī Viṣṇu upon the coils of Ananta-Śeṣa, floating upon the Garbhodaka Samudra—the womb-ocean of fluid, dark salila mixed with the subtle seeds of creation. The dynamics of this ocean are not chaotic but perfectly ordered: its waters are neither fully liquid nor solid but the primal medium that sustains the egg. The Brahmanda floats upon it as a luminous orb, half-submerged, its lower hemisphere bathed in the Garbhodaka while its upper regions rise into the airy spaces. From the navel of Garbhodakaśāyī Viṣṇu springs the lotus of creation upon which Brahmāji is born, and from him the fourteen lokas unfold. Thus the Garbhodaka is both cradle and support, its fluid dynamics governed by the Tattvic waves of the Lord’s breath.

### 2. Vertical & Horizontal Architecture: The Fourteen Lokas and Bhu-Maṇḍala

**The Fourteen Lokas** form the vertical axis of the Brahmanda, graded by subtlety—from the dense, tamasic density of Pātāla to the pure sattva of Satyaloka. Their presiding Rishis and distances (in yojanas) are as revealed in the Puranic streams and affirmed in the Ramayana corpus:

- **Lower Seven (Pātāla-lokas):** Atala, Vitala, Sutala, Talātala, Mahātala, Rasātala, Pātāla—each approximately 10,000 yojanas in thickness and situated progressively below Bhu-maṇḍala. Here dwell the Dānavas and Nāgas in jewel-lit splendour; sunlight does not reach, yet the hood-gems of the great serpents provide eternal illumination. Presiding: the Asura lords under the subtle governance of Śeṣa-nāga.
- **Upper Seven (Vyāhṛti-lokas):** 
  - Bhūloka (part of Bhu-maṇḍala)—our plane.
  - Bhuvarloka (space between earth and sun).
  - Svargaloka (realm of Indra and Devas).
  - Maharloka (abode of great Rishis, 10,000,000 yojanas above Dhruvaloka).
  - Janaloka (realm of the progenitors, 20,000,000 yojanas above Maharloka).
  - Tapoloka (austerity realm of Vaibhrāja Rishis, 80,000,000 yojanas above Janaloka).
  - Satyaloka (Brahmaloka, abode of Brahmā, 120,000,000 yojanas above Tapoloka).

The subtlety gradient is absolute: Pātāla is heavy with tamas and rajas; Satyaloka is almost pure sattva, where time itself is nearly suspended and the Rishis behold the Lord directly. In the Ramayana streams—particularly the Yogavāsiṣṭha—these lokas are also seen as states of consciousness within the cosmic mind, yet their objective distances and rulers remain fixed as per the Ādi-Ramayana and Siddhantas.

**Bhu-Maṇḍala** is the horizontal flat plane at the centre of the Brahmanda, conceived as the pericarp of a vast lotus. It comprises the Sapta-dvīpa—seven concentric islands:

1. Jambūdvīpa (central, our known realm, divided into nine varṣas by eight mountains, with Meru at the heart).
2. Plakṣadvīpa.
3. Śālmali-dvīpa.
4. Kuśadvīpa.
5. Krauñcadvīpa.
6. Śākadvīpa.
7. Puṣkaradvīpa.

Each dvīpa is encircled by an ocean twice its breadth, in perfect geometric order (as per Sthapatyaveda):

- Lavaṇa Samudra (salt water) around Jambū.
- Ikṣu-rasa Samudra (cane-juice).
- Surā Samudra (wine).
- Ghṛta Samudra (ghee).
- Dadhi Samudra (yogurt).
- Kṣīra Samudra (milk).
- Jala Samudra (sweet water).

The entire Bhu-maṇḍala spans vast yojanas, its geometry encoding the divine proportions of creation.

### 3. The Śiśumāra Mechanism: The Celestial Dolphin as Engine of the Stars

At the axis of all planetary motion stands the Śiśumāra Chakra—the celestial dolphin constellation. This great machine, visible as the coiled form of stars and planets, is itself a form of the Lord (sometimes identified with Vāsudeva). Its head is downward; its body coils like a serpent. At the tip of the tail resides Dhruva-loka—the Polestar—where the great devotee Dhruva Mahārāja shines eternally, the pivot of the universe.

The seven great sages (Saptarṣi) form the waist; the planets of Agni, Indra, Prajāpati, Kaśyapa and Dharma adorn the body. All stars and planets are tethered by the Pravaha-Vāyu—the cosmic winds that issue from the Lord’s breath and whirl the entire system around Dhruva in perfect synchrony. The Śiśumāra is the engine: without the tethering winds and the polestar’s stability, the orbits would collapse. Yogīs meditate upon this form as Kuṇḍalinī-cakra, beholding it as the visible virāṭ-puruṣa. This mechanism is affirmed identically in the Saurya and Chandra Ramayanas through their solar-lunar alignments and in the Vasiṣṭha Siddhānta.

### 4. Cosmic Time Dilation: From Paramaṇu to the Life of Brahmā

Time is relative to the loka and the guṇa. The smallest unit is the Paramaṇu (atom of time). From this arise the larger measures:

- One Mahāyuga (Caturyuga) = 4,320,000 human years (Satya 1,728,000; Tretā 1,296,000; Dvāpara 864,000; Kali 432,000).
- One Manvantara = 71 Mahāyugas + sandhyās.
- One Kalpa (day of Brahmā) = 1,000 Mahāyugas = 4.32 billion human years (14 Manvantaras + 15 sandhyās).
- Brahmā’s full day-night = 8.64 billion years.
- Brahmā’s lifespan (as per the Brahma-Ramayana and Puranic consensus) = 100 divine years = 36,000 Kalpas ≈ 311.04 trillion human years.

At the end of each Kalpa comes partial dissolution (naimittika-pralaya); at the end of Brahmā’s life, the mahā-pralaya dissolves all Brahmandas back into the Karana Samudra. In the Yogavāsiṣṭha this dilation is contemplated as the dream of the Supreme, where one human year equals eons in lower lokas and moments in higher. Subtle variations across Kalpas affect only the lifespan of beings within the lokas, not the cosmic architecture.

### 5. The Elemental Shells: From Prithvī to Mahat and the Transition to Paravyoma

Encasing the fourteen lokas is the Brahmanda shell itself, composed of eight concentric layers of increasing subtlety and thickness (each ten times the previous, as per the Āgamas and Bhāgavatam):

1. Prithvī (earth element).
2. Ap (water).
3. Tejas (fire).
4. Vāyu (air).
5. Ākāśa (ether).
6. Ahankāra-tattva (false ego).
7. Mahat-tattva (total material energy).

These Tattvic shells form the crust of the Cosmic Egg. Beyond the final layer of mahat-tattva lies the transition into Paravyoma—the spiritual sky of Vaikuṇṭha and Goloka, where the material guṇas cease and the eternal līlā of the Lord unfolds. The shells are not mere barriers but graduated veils of Māyā, penetrable by the pure devotee or jñānī through the grace of the Rishis.

Thus, O seeker, is the complete map of the Brahmanda—vertical, horizontal, dynamic, temporal, and elemental—as revealed in the Ārṣa tradition. Meditate upon it with reverence; let the Śiśumāra turn in your heart, and Dhruva become your pole. The same structure holds across Kalpas and Ramayana streams, differing only in the inner vision granted to each sage.

May the Lord who reclines on the Garbhodaka bless you with darśana of this eternal truth.
