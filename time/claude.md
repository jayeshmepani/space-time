**A few critical scholarly observations:**

**On your "Paramāṇu as smallest unit" intuition** — you were exactly right, but with an important nuance. Paramāṇu is the smallest unit of *matter*, not technically a unit of *time*. The Bhāgavata Purāṇa (3.11.3) famously uses atomic motion itself to *define* time — "one can estimate time by measuring the movement of the atomic combination of bodies." So the smallest *time* unit is the **Truṭi** (the duration needed for 3 Trasareṇus to integrate), but Paramāṇu is its metaphysical foundation.

**On the two chains diverging below the Muhūrta:**
- The Bhāgavata Purāṇa (Vyāsa) builds an unbroken chain from atomic matter all the way up, giving us 9 time units before the Muhūrta. It is the only text that does this.
- The Viṣṇu Purāṇa (Parāśara), Manusmṛti (Svāyambhuva Manu), and Mahābhārata (Vyāsa) all begin at the Nimeṣa and use a simpler 3-step chain: **Nimeṣa → Kāṣṭhā → Kalā → Muhūrta**. They are in remarkable agreement with each other — the only disagreement among them is that Manusmṛti says **18 Nimeṣas = 1 Kāṣṭhā**, while VP and MBh say **15 Nimeṣas = 1 Kāṣṭhā**.

**On the Tatpara (Row 3a)** — this appears only in the Viṣṇu Purāṇa's alternate sub-system and uses a different definition of Truṭi than BhP. The two cannot be directly compared.

**All four texts are unanimous** from the Muhūrta upward — every unit from Muhūrta → Yāma → Ahorātra → Pakṣa → Māsa → Ṛtu → Ayana → Saṃvatsara is consistent across all four sage-attributed traditions.

## Two independent sub‑muhūrta chains

Both reach the same muhūrta (48 min) and all larger units:

**BhP chain** (Veda Vyāsa · 3.11): matter‑grounded from atomic movement →  
Truṭi (474 μs) → Vedha → Lava → Nimeṣa (0.43 s) → Kṣaṇa → Kāṣṭhā (6.4 s) → Laghu → Nāḍikā (24 min) → Muhūrta (48 min)

**VP / MS / MBh chain**: starts at Nimeṣa (0.18–0.21 s) →  
Kāṣṭhā (3.2 s) → Kalā (96 s) → Muhūrta (48 min)

The two systems share unit names but differ in nimeṣa duration and intermediate steps. Above the muhūrta, all four texts are in full agreement.
