# Hindu Ārṣa Chronometry: Complete Reference

## Scope
This document presents a complete synthesis of Hindu temporal metrology from primary sources. Multiple texts use the same unit-names with different magnitudes — the most important case being the Bhāgavata chain versus the Manu and Viṣṇu chains. In addition, some texts explicitly present multiple coexisting "measures" (*māna*) for different applications (astronomy, ritual, lunar observances, etc.), and those must not be forced into a single unit-ladder.

## Synthesis Rules
1. `Bhāgavata Purāṇa 3.11` is the only continuous atomic-to-year ladder among the primary sources.
2. `Manusmṛti 1.64-67` and `Viṣṇu Purāṇa I.3.8-10` are parallel ritual/calendrical ladders, not identical to Bhāgavata below the `muhūrta`.
3. Same-name units are kept separate when their magnitudes differ.
4. Modern seconds, minutes, and microseconds are derived values only. The classical verses give ratios, not SI units.
5. Multi-framework presentations (distinct *māna* systems) are handled as their own analytical layer, because they are not a single micro-to-macro metrology chain.

`Manusmṛti 1.64-67`

- `18 nimeṣas = 1 kāṣṭhā`

### 5a. Vedic-Ritual Akṣara (Syllable) Time System
This is a distinct metrological framework where time is measured by performative action (recitation) rather than atomic or calendrical division.

- `akṣara` (syllable) = base unit, time to pronounce one short vowel
- `7200 akṣaras = 1 muhūrta` → derived: `1 akṣara ≈ 0.4 s`
- `1000 bṛhatī verses = 1 year` → `33,000 akṣaras = 1 saṃvatsara`
- this system is rooted in Vedic recitation practice and bridges ritual performance with temporal measurement
- phonetic calibration: 60 Gurvakṣaras (long syllables) recited in exactly 24 seconds, used to regulate the Ghaṭī Yantra (water clock)

### 5b. Ayurvedic Time Chains (Sage-Attributed Medical Texts)
Two additional sage-attributed sources define time units for medical and physiological timing, providing alternative starting points below the nimeṣa.

**Caraka Saṃhitā** (traditionally Agniveśa, revised by Caraka; Ṛṣi Punarvasu Ātreya lineage)
- `Sūtrasthāna 4/5`: gives akṣi-nimeṣa (mātra) → kāṣṭhā → kalā → nāḍikā → muhūrta → yāma → ahorātra → pakṣa → māsa → ṛtu → ayana → varṣa
- akṣi-nimeṣa = time to blink the eyelids, identified as the smallest unit in this system
- 15 mātras = 1 kāṣṭhā; 30 kāṣṭhās = 1 kalā; 20 + 1/10 kalās = 1 nāḍikā; 2 nāḍikās = 1 muhūrta

**Suśruta Saṃhitā** (traditionally taught by Dhanvantari, an avatāra, to Suśruta)
- `Sūtrasthāna 6/4`: defines akṣi-nimeṣa → kāṣṭhā → muhūrta → ahorātra → pakṣa → māsa → ṛtu → ayana → saṃvatsara
- akṣi-nimeṣa = time taken to articulate a short vowel
- 15 akṣi-nimeṣas = 1 kāṣṭhā; 30 kāṣṭhās = 1 kalā; 30 kalās = 1 muhūrta; 30 muhūrtas = 1 ahorātra

### 5c. Atharvaveda Kālasūkta (Time as Cosmological Principle)
The Atharvaveda establishes time (Kāla) as the supreme organizing principle of the cosmos, providing the philosophical foundation within which all Purāṇic time units operate.

- `Atharvaveda 19.53.1`: Kāla described as a primordial horse with seven reins (rays) and a thousand eyes
- `Atharvaveda 19.53-54`: Kālasūkta — earliest Vedic treatment of eternal time as the supreme principle
- this does not define sub-units but establishes the cosmological framework for all later metrology

### 6. Nārada Purāṇa Multi-*Māna* Framework (Five Practical Reckonings)
This layer is not a replacement for the unit ladders above; it is a taxonomy of distinct time-reckonings and their use-cases.

`Nārada Purāṇa (Pūrvārdha) 1.56.109-135`

### D. Distinct *Māna* Systems (Not One Ladder): Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.109-135
Multiple legitimate time-reckonings (*māna*) coexist, each chosen by function. This is the kind of layer that becomes confused if we force "time" into a single chain.

#### D.1 The nine *māna* (measures) and the practical subset
The text lists nine kinds of measure (*māna*): `brahma`, `deva`, `manu`, `pitṛ`, `saura`, `sāvana`, `cāndra`, `ārkṣa`, and `guru` (Bṛhaspati/Jupiter-based) (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.109*). Immediately after, it states that practical worldly "business" (*vyavahāra*) proceeds through five of these, and then promises a per-measure account of their distinct tasks (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.110*).

"Time" is not only a ladder of unit-multipliers, but also a selection of a reckoning framework suited to the question being asked.

#### D.2 Assigned use-cases: why different reckonings are not interchangeable
The chapter assigns use-cases:

- Solar measure (*saura*) for the total motion/going of the planets (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.111*).
- Civil measure (*sāvana*) for the rule of rains and pregnancy (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.111*).
- Nakṣatra-based determination is explicitly invoked in the same practical domain (rainfall/pregnancy timing), while tithi-based decision-making is invoked for journeys, marriage, vows, shaving rites, and “year-lord” decisions (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.112*).
- Lunar measure (*cāndra*) governs parva, vāstu, upavāsa, and the general class of lunar observances (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.112*).
- Jupiter-based measure (*guru-māna*) governs the year-character beginning with `Prabhava` etc. (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.113*).

Once a text says "these nine exist, but practice uses five, and each has its own work", then "same month name" or "same year word" does not automatically mean "same calendar basis".

#### D.3 Bārhaspatya (Jovian) year-cycle: the 60 named *saṃvatsara*
The chapter explicitly defines a 60-year cycle of named years beginning with `Prabhava` (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.114*) and then lists the 60 names across *Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.115-120*. It then states that all sixty years are to be understood as having results corresponding to their names, and that a "yuga" is five years and there are twelve such yugas within the sixty-year cycle, along with enumerated "lords" (*īśa*) for those yugas (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.121-123*).

This section is calendrical (classification of year-names and their lords), not metrological (truṭi-to-day), so it belongs to a separate layer alongside the ladders above.

#### D.4 Ayanas, seasons, month-taxonomy, month names, and pakṣa semantics
The chapter states the six-month northern course (*saumya-ayana*) and the southern course beginning with `karka` (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.125*), and links them to the gods’ day and night (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.125*). It specifies that auspicious rites are performed in uttarāyaṇa, while dakṣiṇāyaṇa is treated oppositely in ritual valuation (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.126-127*).

It also defines the six ṛtu as two-month pairs starting with Māgha and groups them into the uttarāyaṇa and dakṣiṇāyaṇa halves (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.127-128*).

Then it distinguishes month-bases: the lunar month bounded by new moon, the solar month beginning from *saṅkrānti*, and the nakṣatra month (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.129*). It lists the 12 month-names from `Madhu` onward (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.130*) and gives the month naming principle: the month is named after the nakṣatra with which the full moon is conjoined in that month (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.131*). Finally, it defines the two pakṣa (śukla/kṛṣṇa) as "daiva" and "pitṛ" and links them to auspicious vs inauspicious ritual usage (*Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.132*).

#### D.5 Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa Time-Segment Qualitative Taxonomy
The Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa (3.10) goes beyond the quantitative definition of time units and assigns **qualitative names** to day-times, night-times, and muhūrtas. The text provides multiple naming systems (TB 3.10.1.1-3 for day-times/night-times, TB 3.10.1.1-4 for muhūrtas), establishing that time segments carry distinct energetic signatures.

**Naming Scheme A — Day-Portion Names** (bright fortnight; TB 3.10.1.1-3):
`saṃjñānaṃ, vijñānaṃ, prajñānaṃ, jānad, abhijānad, saṃkalpamānaṃ, prakalpamānaṃ, upakalpamānaṃ, upakṛptamānaṃ, kṛpta, śreya, vaśiya, āyat, sambhūta, bhūta`

**Naming Scheme A — Night-Portion Names** (bright fortnight; TB 3.10.1.1-3):
`darśa, dṛṣṭā, darśatā, viśvarūpa, sudarśanā, āpyāyamānā, pyāyamānā, pyāyā, sunṛtā, īrā, apūryamāṇā, pūryamāṇā, pūrayantī, pūrṇā, paurṇamāsī`

**Naming Scheme B — Daylight Muhūrta Names** (bright fortnight; TB 3.10.1.1-4):
`citrā, ketuḥ, prabhā, nabhan, sambhan, jyotiṣmān, tejasvān, ātapan, tapan, abhitapan, rocanā, rocāmānaḥ, śobhanā, śobhamānaḥ, kalyāṇaḥ`

**Naming Scheme B — Night Muhūrta Names** (bright fortnight; TB 3.10.1.1-4):
`savitā, prasavitā, diptā, dīpāyan, dīpyamānaḥ, jvalan, jvalitā, tapan, vitapan, śāntapan, rocanā, rocāmānaḥ, śumbhuḥ, śumbhamānaḥ, vāmaḥ`

Naming Scheme A assigns names to the day-portion and night-portion of the bright fortnight; Naming Scheme B assigns names to each of the 15 individual muhūrtas within the daylight hours and the 15 individual muhūrtas within the night hours. The daylight muhūrtas progress through luminosity and radiant energy (Citrā → Kalyāṇaḥ); the night muhūrtas govern illumination, burning, and pacification (Savitā → Vāmaḥ). This qualitative mapping establishes that the 30 muhūrtas of a day are **not interchangeable slots** but carry distinct character and intent, aligning human action (karma) with appropriate temporal windows.

## What Must Not Be Blurred

### 1. Different `nimeṣa` magnitudes
- `Nimeṣa (BhP)` = `3 lavas` = `426.6667 ms`
- `Nimeṣa (Manu)` = `1/18 kāṣṭhā` = `177.7778 ms`
- `Nimeṣa (VP)` = `1/15 kāṣṭhā` = `213.3333 ms`

### 2. Different `kāṣṭhā` magnitudes
- `Kāṣṭhā (BhP)` = `5 kṣaṇas` = `6.4 s`
- `Kāṣṭhā (Manu / VP)` = `3.2 s`

### 3. Equal magnitude, different name/path
- `Bhāgavata laghu = 96 s`
- `Manu / VP kalā = 96 s`
- same derived magnitude, different chain position and different textual role

### 4. Sūrya Siddhānta is not the same ladder
- `SS 1.11-13` gives `prāṇa`, `vināḍī`, `nāḍikā`, `ahorātra`, `māsa`, `varṣa`
- this document does not use it to overwrite Bhāgavata, Manu, or Viṣṇu values

### 5. Corroboration does not mean identical magnitude
- a text may confirm a unit-name without matching another text's exact sub-chain
- therefore corroboration is useful, but only after checking whether the unit is truly the same in magnitude and position

### 6. Different calendar-frameworks can coexist without contradiction
- a text may discuss not just smaller and larger units, but distinct `māna` systems meant for different functions
- separate calendar frameworks should not be merged into a single unit-ladder unless their exact verses are also reproduced and compared
- `Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.109-113` explicitly states multiple coexisting measures with assigned use-cases; treat it as a separate analytical layer, not as a "missing rung" in the ladder

### 7. The akṣara (syllable) system is not a sub-chain of the atomic ladder
- `akṣara` = ~0.4 s derived from 7200 akṣaras per muhūrta
- this is a **performative** unit based on Vedic recitation speed, not an atomic or physiological division
- it belongs to the ritual-metrical framework, not the metrological chain
- phonetic calibration (60 Gurvakṣaras = 24 s) was used to regulate water clocks independently of atomic or calendrical measures

### 8. Ayurvedic chains start at akṣi-nimeṣa, not paramāṇu
- `Caraka Saṃhitā Sū. 4/5` and `Suśruta Saṃhitā Sū. 6/4` define time starting from the blink of the eye (akṣi-nimeṣa / mātra)
- these are sage-attributed medical texts using physiological timing for clinical and pharmacological purposes
- they do not include the paramāṇu → aṇu → trasareṇu → truṭi → vedha → lava sequence found in BhP 3.11
- treat them as a separate analytical layer focused on human-scale temporal perception

### 9. Muhūrtas carry qualitative names, not only quantitative value
- `Taittirīya Brāhmaṇa 3.10.1.1-4` assigns distinct qualitative names to each of the 15 daytime muhūrtas (citrā → kalyāṇaḥ) and 15 nighttime muhūrtas (savitā → vāmaḥ)
- TB 3.10.1.1-3 additionally assigns separate names to day-times (saṃjñānaṃ → bhūta) and night-times (darśa → paurṇamāsī) for the bright fortnight
- this establishes that time segments are **not interchangeable** but carry distinct energetic character
- the 30 muhūrtas of a day are qualitatively differentiated, affecting ritual suitability and action timing

## Appendix A2: Non-Sage-Attributed / Syncretic & Macro Units

This appendix collects time units and subdivisions that appear in later pedagogical compilations, mathematical texts, and civil/astrological conventions. **None of these belong to the sage-attributed metrological chains** (BhP, Manu, VP, SS). They are documented here because they appear in modern charts and panchāṅga practice, and their presence in those contexts must be explicitly distinguished from the Ārṣa textual sources.

### A2.1 Sidereal Units (Commentarial Tradition)

A 60-fold sexagesimal sub-muhūrta chain appears in later commentarial and pedagogical sources. **This is not from Sūrya Siddhānta.** The Sūrya Siddhānta (1.11) defines only Prāṇa → Vināḍī → Nāḍikā — it does not define this 60-fold ladder below the prāṇa. The units and multipliers below are reconstructed from later astronomical commentary:

| Unit | Definition | Value in SI Units |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Truṭi (त्रुटि) | base unit | ≈ 308.6 ns |
| Reṇu (रेणु) | 60 Truṭi | ≈ 18.5 μs |
| Lava (लव) | 60 Reṇu | ≈ 1,111 μs |
| Līkṣaka (लीक्षक) | 60 Lava | ≈ 66.66 ms |
| Liptā (लिप्ता) / Vipala (विपल) | 6 Līkṣaka | ≈ 0.4 s |
| Prāṇa (प्राण) | 10 Liptā | ≈ 4 s |
| Pala (पल) / Vighaṭi (विघटि) / Vināḍī (विनाडी) | 60 Liptā or 6 Prāṇa | ≈ 24 s |
| Ghaṭi (घटि) / Nāḍī (नाडी) / Daṇḍa (दण्ड) | 60 Vighaṭi | ≈ 1.44 ks (24 min) |
| Muhūrta (मुहूर्त) | 2 Ghaṭi | ≈ 2.88 ks (48 min) |
| Nakṣatra Ahorātram (नक्षत्र अहोरात्रम्; sidereal day) | 60 Ghaṭī or 30 Muhūrta | ≈ 86.4 ks (24 h) |

**Status:** Textually unattested as a continuous ladder in any single canonical śāstra. The terms are drawn from disparate sources (Truṭi from SS, Reṇu from Jain/cosmological texts, Lava from Purāṇas, Līkṣaka from Bhāskarācārya's Līlāvatī as a distance unit, not a time unit) and harmonized into a uniform 60× chain by later commentators.

### A2.2 Siddhānta Śiromaṇi — Mathematical Computation Chain

Bhāskarācārya's *Siddhānta Śiromaṇi* (12th century CE) provides a 30-fold mathematical hierarchy used in the *Madhyamādhikāra* (Chapter on Mean Longitudes) for astronomical computation. This chain uses different multipliers and values from the Purāṇic chains:

| Unit | Definition | Value in SI Units |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Truṭi | base unit | ≈ 29.6 μs |
| Tatpara | 100 Truṭi | ≈ 2.96 ms |
| Nimeṣa | 30 Tatpara | ≈ 88.9 ms |
| Kāṣṭhā (काष्ठा) | 18 Nimeṣa | ≈ 1.6 s |
| Kalā (कला) | 30 Kāṣṭhā | ≈ 48 s |
| Ghaṭikā | 30 Kalā | ≈ 1.44 ks (24 min) |
| Muhūrta (मुहूर्त) / Kṣaṇa (क्षण) | 2 Ghaṭikā | ≈ 2.88 ks (48 min) |
| Ahorātram (sidereal day) | 30 Muhūrta | ≈ 86.4 ks (24 h) |

**Source:** Bhāskarācārya, *Siddhānta Śiromaṇi*, Madhyamādhikāra. This chain converges at the muhūrta (48 min) with the Purāṇic chains but diverges completely in the sub-nimeṣa range. It is a **computational tool for planetary mean longitudes**, not a biological or metrological ladder.

### A2.3 Macro-Units (Civil & Astrological)

These are larger time units that appear in civil practice, panchāṅga tradition, and later astrological convention:

| Unit | Composition | Derived Value | Status |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Coghadiyā (चोघडिया) | 1.875 Muhūrta ≈ 1.5 hours | 1.5 hr | **Later astrological construct** — derived from "Chau-Ghaḍī" (4 Ghaṭikās, where 4 × 24m = 96m ≈ 1.5h). Used for auspicious timing (muhūrta-śāstra), not found in canonical Ārṣa metrology. |
| Saptāha (सप्ताह) | 7 Ahorātra | 7 days | **Historical/civil unit** — the 7-day week (Vāra) is a historical and civil convention, not foundational to the Ārṣa atomic framework. The seven Vāras are named after the seven grahas (Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn). |

## Appendix C: Sanskrit Verses Covering All Citations Used Above

### I. Bhāgavata Purāṇa 3.11

**BhP 3.11.1**
> मैत्रेय उवाच
> चरमः सद्विशेषाणामनेकोऽसंयुतः सदा ।
> परमाणुः स विज्ञेयो नृणामैक्यभ्रमो यतः ॥ १ ॥

*Translation*: "Maitreya said: The ultimate particle of the material manifestation, which is indivisible and never combined, is called the atom (paramāṇu). It should be known as the source of people's confusion about unity."

**BhP 3.11.4**
> स कालः परमाणुर्वै यो भुङ्क्ते परमाणुताम् ।
> सतोऽविशेषभुग्यस्तु स कालः परमो महान् ॥ ४ ॥

*Translation*: "That time which consumes the atomic state is the supreme great time, which consumes the undifferentiated state of being."

**BhP 3.11.5**
> अणुर्द्वौ परमाणु स्यात्त्रसरेणुस्त्रयः स्मृतः ।
> जालार्करश्म्यवगतः खमेवानुपतन्नगात् ॥ ५ ॥

*Translation*: "Two atoms make one double atom, and three double atoms make one hexatom. This is observed in the sun's rays passing through the holes of a lattice, as they move through the sky."

**BhP 3.11.6**
> त्रसरेणुत्रिकं भुङ्क्ते यः कालः स त्रुटिः स्मृतः ।
> शतभागस्तु वेधः स्यात्तैस्त्रिभिस्तु लवः स्मृतः ॥ ६ ॥

*Translation*: "The time that consumes three hexatoms is called one truṭi. One hundredth of that is called vedha, and three vedhas make one lava."

**BhP 3.11.7**
> निमेषस्त्रिलवो ज्ञेय आम्नातस्ते त्रयः क्षणः ।
> क्षणान्पञ्च विदुः काष्ठां लघु ता दश पञ्च च ॥ ७ ॥

*Translation*: "One nimeṣa should be known as three lavas. Three nimeṣas are called one kṣaṇa. Five kṣaṇas make one kāṣṭhā, and fifteen kāṣṭhās make one laghu."

**BhP 3.11.8**
> लघूनि वै समाम्नाता दश पञ्च च नाडिका ।
> ते द्वे मुहूर्तः प्रहरः षड्यामः सप्त वा नृणाम् ॥ ८ ॥

*Translation*: "Fifteen laghus make one nāḍikā. Two nāḍikās make one muhūrta. The day and night of mortals consists of six or seven praharas (yāmas)."

**BhP 3.11.9**
> द्वादशार्धपलोन्मानं चतुर्भिश्चतुरङ्गुलैः ।
> स्वर्णमाषैः कृतच्छिद्रं यावत्प्रस्थजलप्लुतम् ॥ ९ ॥

*Translation*: "A measure of twelve and a half palas, with a hole made by four fingers of gold māṣas, is filled up to one prastha of water." *(This describes the water-clock mechanism for measuring one nāḍikā.)*

**BhP 3.11.10**
> यामाश्चत्वारश्चत्वारो मर्त्यानामहनी उभे ।
> पक्षः पञ्चदशाहानि शुक्लः कृष्णश्च मानद ॥ १० ॥

*Translation*: "Four yāmas each make the two halves (day and night) of mortals. A pakṣa consists of fifteen days — the bright fortnight and the dark fortnight, O giver of measure."

**BhP 3.11.11**
> तयोः समुच्चयो मासः पितॄणां तदहर्निशम् ।
> द्वौ तावृतुः षडयनं दक्षिणं चोत्तरं दिवि ॥ ११ ॥

*Translation*: "The combination of those two (pakṣas) makes one month, which is one day and night of the pitṛs. Two months make one ṛtu; six ṛtus make one ayana — the southern and the northern, in the celestial sphere."

**BhP 3.11.12**
> अयने चाहनी प्राहुर्वत्सरो द्वादश स्मृतः ।
> संवत्सरशतं नृणां परमायुर्निरूपितम् ॥ १२ ॥

*Translation*: "The two ayanas make one day and night (of the gods). Twelve months are called one year. One hundred saṃvatsaras are established as the maximum lifespan of humans."

### II. Manusmṛti 1.64-67

**Manu 1.64**
> निमेषा दश चाष्टौ च काष्ठा त्रिंशत् तु ताः कला ।
> त्रिंशत् कला मुहूर्तः स्यादहोरात्रं तु तावतः ॥ ६४ ॥

*Translation*: "Ten and eight nimeṣas make one kāṣṭhā; thirty kāṣṭhās make one kalā; thirty kalās make one muhūrta; and one day-and-night (ahorātra) consists of that many (thirty muhūrtas)."

**Manu 1.65**
> अहोरात्रे विभजते सूर्यो मानुषदैविके ।
> रात्रिः स्वप्नाय भूतानां चेष्टायै कर्मणामहः ॥ ६५ ॥

*Translation*: "The sun divides the day and night into human and divine. Night is for the sleep of beings; the day is for the performance of actions."

**Manu 1.66**
> पित्र्ये रात्र्यहनी मासः प्रविभागस्तु पक्षयोः ।

*Translation*: "For the pitṛs (ancestors), a month is the division of their day and night, split between the two fortnights. The dark fortnight (kṛṣṇa pakṣa) is their day for activity; the bright night (śukla pakṣa) is for their sleep."

**Manu 1.67**
> दैवे रात्र्यहनी वर्षं प्रविभागस्तयोः पुनः ।

*Translation*: "For the gods, a year is the division of their day and night. There, the northern solstice (udagayana) is their day; the southern solstice (dakṣiṇāyana) is their night."

*Translation*: "Piṅgala, Kālayukta, Siddhārtha, Raudra, Durmatī, Dundubhi, Rudhirodgārī, Raktākṣa, Krodhana, Kṣaya." *(Year-names fifty-one through sixty.)*

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.121**
> नामतुल्यफलाः सर्वे विज्ञेयाः षष्टिवत्सराः।
> युगं स्थात्पंचभिर्वर्षैर्युगान्येवं तु द्वादश ॥ १२१ ॥

*Translation*: "All sixty years are to be known by results corresponding to their names. A yuga is established by five years; and there are twelve such yugas (in the sixty-year cycle)."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.122**
> तेषामीशाः क्रमाज्ज्ञेया विष्णुर्देवपुरोहितः।
> पुरंदरो लोहितश्च त्वष्टाहिर्बुध्न्यसंज्ञकः ॥ १२२ ॥

*Translation*: "The lords of these yugas are to be known in order: Viṣṇu, Devaguru (Bṛhaspati), Purandara (Indra), Lohita (Maṅgala), Tvaṣṭā, Ahi (Rāhu), and Budhnya-saṃjñaka."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.123**
> पितरश्च ततो विश्वे शशींद्रा ग्न्यश्विनो भगः।
> तथा युगस्य वर्षेशास्त्वग्निनेंदुविधीश्वराः ॥ १२३ ॥

*Translation*: "Then the Pitṛs, the Viśvedevas, Soma, Indra, Agni, the Aśvinīs, Bhaga. Thus the year-lords of each yuga: Agni, Soma, and the lord of beings (Prajāpati)."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.124**
> अथाद्वेशचमूनाथसस्यपानां बलाबलम्।
> तत्कालं ग्रहचारं च सम्यग् ज्ञात्वा फलं वदेत् ॥ १२४ ॥

*Translation*: "Then, having correctly known the strength and weakness of the lord of the year, the lord of the army, the crops, and the rains, as well as the planetary motions of that time, one should declare the results."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.125**
> सौम्यायनं मासषट्कं मृगाद्यं भानुभुक्तितः।
> अहः सुराणां तद्रा त्रिः कर्काद्यं दक्षिणायनम् ॥ १२५ ॥

*Translation*: "The six-month northern course beginning with Capricorn, reckoned from the Sun's motion, is the day of the gods; the southern course beginning with Cancer is their night."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.126**
> गृहप्रवेशवैवाहप्रतिष्ठामौंजिबन्धनम्।
> मघादौ मंगलं कर्म विधेयं चोत्तरायणे ॥ १२६ ॥

*Translation*: "House-entering, marriage, consecration, and the sacred-thread ceremony — these auspicious rites are to be performed in the northern course beginning with Maghā."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.127**
> याम्यायने गर्हितं च कर्म यत्नात्प्रशस्यते।
> माघादिमासौ द्वौ द्वौ च ऋतवः शिशिरादयः ॥ १२७ ॥

*Translation*: "In the southern course (dakṣiṇāyana), rites are to be avoided; they are commended with effort in the northern course. The months beginning with Māgha, taken two by two, form the seasons beginning with śiśira."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.128**
> मृगाच्छिशिरवसंतश्च ग्रीष्माः स्युश्चोत्तरायणे।
> वर्षा शरच्च हेमंतः कर्काद्वै दक्षिणायने ॥ १२८ ॥

*Translation*: "From Capricorn, the seasons śiśira and vasanta, and then grīṣma, are in the northern course. Varṣā, śarad, and hemanta are in the southern course beginning with Cancer."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.129**
> चांद्रो दर्शावधिः सौरः संक्रात्या सावनो दिनैः।
> त्रिंशद्भिश्चंद्र भगणो मासो नाक्षत्रसंज्ञकः ॥ १२९ ॥

*Translation*: "The lunar month is bounded by new moon (darśa); the solar month begins with saṅkrānti; the civil month is by days (sāvana); the month of the Moon's revolution is called the nakṣatra month."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.130**
> मधुश्च माधवः शुक्रः शुचिश्चाथ नभस्ततः।
> नभस्य इषः ऊर्जश्च सहाश्चैव सहस्यकः ॥ १३० ॥
> तपास्तपस्य क्रमशश्चैत्रादीनां समाह्वयाः।

*Translation*: "Madhu, Mādhava, Śukra, Śuci, Nabhas, Nabhasya, Iṣa, Ūrja, Saha, Sahasyaka, Tapas, Tapasya — these are the respective names of the months beginning with Caitra."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.131**
> यस्मिन्मासे पौर्णमासी येन धिष्ण्येन संयुता।
> तन्नक्षत्राह्वयो मासः पौर्णमासी तदाह्वया ॥ १३१ ॥

*Translation*: "In whichever month the full-moon is conjoined with a particular nakṣatra, that month is named after that nakṣatra, and the full-moon (too) is so called."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.132**
> तत्पक्षौ दैव पित्राख्यौ शुक्लकृष्णौ तथापरे।
> शुभाशुभे कर्मणि च प्रशस्तौ भवतः सदा ॥ १३२ ॥

*Translation*: "Of its two fortnights, one is called divine (daiva) and the other ancestral (pitṛ); these are the bright (śukla) and dark (kṛṣṇa). They are always commended for auspicious and inauspicious rites respectively."

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.133**
> क्रमात्तिथीनां ब्रह्माग्नी विरिंचिविष्णुशैलजाः ॥ १३३ ॥

*Translation*: "In order, the lords of the tithis are: Brahmā, Agni, Viriñci, Viṣṇu, Śailajā..." *(Lords of tithis 1-5.)*

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.134**
> विनायकयमौ नागचंद्रौ स्कंदोऽकवासवौ।
> महेन्द्र वासवौ नागदुर्गादंडधराह्वयः ॥ १३४ ॥

*Translation*: "Vināyaka, Yama, the Serpent, the Moon, Skanda, the Sun, Vāsava, Mahendra, Vāsava, the Serpent, Durgā, Daṇḍadhara — these are the lords." *(Lords of tithis 6-15.)*

**Nārada Purāṇa 1.56.135**
> शिवविष्णू हरिरवीकामः सर्वः कलीततः।
> चन्द्र विश्वेदर्शसंज्ञतिथीशाः पितरः स्मृताः ॥ १३५ ॥

*Translation*: "Śiva, Viṣṇu, Hari, Ravī, Kāma, Sarva, Kalī, and thereafter Soma, the Viśvedevas, and Darśa — these are the lords of the dark-fortnight tithis, remembered as the Pitṛs."

## Final Synthesis Statement
Hindu temporal metrology does not present one flat ladder where all names have one universal value. The proper reading of the sources is:

- `Bhāgavata` preserves the full atomic-to-year chain.
- `Manu` and `Viṣṇu` preserve parallel ritual chains from `nimeṣa` to `muhūrta` and upward.
- The names overlap, but some magnitudes do not.
- The strongest synthesis is therefore comparative and convergent, not homogenizing.
