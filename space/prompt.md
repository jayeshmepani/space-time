       Each of these sites is a major digital repository for Sanskrit literature and Indian heritage, but they serve different purposes. Here is a breakdown of their primary focus and "full
    site" functions: [1]
       ## 1. WisdomLib ([wisdomlib.org](https://www.wisdomlib.org/))

       * Focus: A vast encyclopedia for Buddhism, Hinduism, and Jainism.
       * Key Features: It is best known for its searchable Purana and Itihasa index, cross-referencing thousands of terms across texts like the Mahabharata and various Puranas. It also
    provides detailed definitions and academic articles on Indian history and philosophy. [2, 3, 4, 5]

       ## 2. Vedabase (vedabase.io)

       * Focus: The primary online tool for studying the works of A.C. Bhaktivedanta Swami Prabhupada (founder of ISKCON).
       * Key Features: It contains the complete Bhagavad-gita As It Is, Srimad-Bhagavatam, and Caitanya-caritamrta with original Sanskrit, transliteration, word-for-word meanings, and
    detailed purports. It is highly structured for verse-by-verse study. [6, 7, 8, 9, 10]

       ## 3. Sanskrit Documents (sanskritdocuments.org)

       * Focus: A massive, volunteer-run collection of Sanskrit e-texts in various scripts.
       * Key Features: It offers thousands of Stotras, Shlokas, and major works (like the Ramayana) available in Devanagari, IAST, and regional Indian scripts. It also hosts learning tools,
    dictionaries, and links to scanned ancient books. [11, 12, 13, 14, 15]

       ## 4. Vedic Reserve (vedicreserve.miu.edu)

       * Focus: Hosted by Maharishi International University (MIU), this site focuses on the Vedic Literature in Sanskrit.
       * Key Features: It provides high-quality PDF versions of the Rig Veda, Yajur Veda, Sama Veda, and Atharva Veda, along with Upanishads and Brahmanas. The site is designed to support
    Maharishi Mahesh Yogi's philosophy of "reading Vedic literature" to train brain physiology. [16, 17, 18, 19, 20]

       ## 5. Transliteral ([transliteral.org](https://www.transliteral.org/))

       * Focus: A digital library and Unified Dictionary for ancient Indian literature.
       * Key Features: It features a unique dictionary that maps words across Marathi, Hindi, English, and Sanskrit. It also hosts a large collection of Marathi literature and various
    regional religious texts. [21, 22, 23]
