### 🔍 Critical Textual Notes

1. **Attribution Framework**: All cited texts are traditionally Ṛṣi-authored:
   - `Vedāṅga Jyotiṣa` → Sage Lāgaḍha
   - `Manusmṛti` → Sage Manu
   - `Mahābhārata` & `Purāṇas` → Sage Vyāsa (Kṛṣṇa Dvaipāyana)
   - These fall under your `Ārṣa/Śruti-Smṛti` definition, despite modern critical dating placing their redaction between ~800 BCE–400 CE.

2. **Textual Variations**: 
   - Some recensions of `Manusmṛti` (e.g., Kullūka Bhaṭṭa's commentary) give `18 nimeṣa = 1 kāṣṭhā` instead of `15`, but the `30 muhūrta = 1 day` and `360-day year` remain constant across all Ārṣa sources.
   - The sub-nimeṣa sequence (paramāṇu → lava) is most systematically preserved in `Viṣṇu Purāṇa` 1.3 and `Bhāgavata Purāṇa` 3.11, both attributed to Vyāsa.

3. **360-Day Year Context**: 
   - This is the `सवन वर्ष` (Sāvana Varṣa), a civil/ritual year used for Vedic calendrics, yajña scheduling, and dharmaśāstric law. It is distinct from the sidereal (`नाक्षत्र`) or tropical (`सायन`) solar years (~365.25 days), which are discussed in later Pauruṣeya Jyotiṣa.

4. **Modern Equivalents**: 
   - These are derived from the traditional equation: `30 muhūrta = 1200 min = 24 hrs`. The sub-nimeṣa units are proportional and cosmological rather than chronometric; ancient texts treat them as conceptual divisions of consciousness, ritual timing, and cosmic vibration, not stopwatch measurements.
