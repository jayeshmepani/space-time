I checked the verse references you listed, and the biggest issue is that some of the units are **right in substance but assigned to the wrong verse number**, and Appendix B mixes in later, non-sage-attributed material. In *Bhāgavata Purāṇa* 3.11, verse 5 gives **paramāṇu → aṇu → trasareṇu**, verse 6 gives **truṭi → vedha → lava**, verse 7 gives **nimeṣa → kṣaṇa → kāṣṭhā → laghu**, verse 8 gives **nāḍikā/ghaṭikā, muhūrta, prahara/yāma**, verse 9 defines the nāḍikā by the water-clock, verse 10 gives **day/night and pakṣa**, verse 11 gives **month, ṛtu, ayana**, verse 12 gives **year**, and verse 14 lists the different year-names. So the sequence is real, but some of the last-column verse labels in your Appendix A need shifting. ([Wisdom Library][1])

The important interpretive point is that **Bhāgavata is the only one of your cited sources that gives the full ladder from the smallest unit up to year in one continuous chain**. Also, its scale is not identical to the other texts: in Bhāgavata, **5 kṣaṇa = 1 kāṣṭhā** and **15 kāṣṭhā = 1 laghu**, while in *Manusmṛti* and *Viṣṇu Purāṇa* the kāṣṭhā is a different-sized unit. So the names overlap, but the chains are not fully interchangeable. ([Wisdom Library][1])

For *Manusmṛti* 1.64, the citation is correct for the middle-scale ladder: it says **18 nimeṣa = 1 kāṣṭhā**, **30 kāṣṭhā = 1 kalā**, **30 kalā = 1 muhūrta**, and **30 muhūrta = day/night**. It does **not** include paramāṇu, aṇu, or trasareṇu, so it supports only the nimeṣa-to-day section of the timeline. ([Wisdom Library][2])

For *Viṣṇu Purāṇa* 1.3.8–10, the references are broadly right and the wording is very clear: **15 nimeṣa = kāṣṭhā**, **30 kāṣṭhā = kalā**, **30 kalā = muhūrta**, **30 muhūrta = day/night**, then **15 days = month**, **6 months = ayana**, and **2 ayanas = year**. This is a valid parallel chain, but again it is **not the same kāṣṭhā-chain** as Bhāgavata or Manu. ([Wisdom Library][3])

For *Sūrya Siddhānta* 1.10–12, the verses checked describe a different ladder: time is divided into **mūrta** and **amūrta**, then **6 prāṇa = vināḍī**, **60 vināḍī = nāḍī**, **60 nāḍī = nakṣatra ahorātra**, and then **30 such days = month**, **12 months = year**. The verses I checked do **not** mention **tatpara**; that belongs to later Siddhāntic mathematical tradition, not to the cited *Sūrya Siddhānta* verses themselves. So Appendix B, as written, is not strict enough for your “sage-attributed only” rule. ([Encycloreader][4])

On the Vedic side, the *Śatapatha Brāhmaṇa* does use **muhūrta** and also speaks of the **twelve months of the year**, but the sources I checked do not show the full paramāṇu-to-year ladder there. So, for a strict arṣa-only canonical sequence, the cleanest full list is **Bhāgavata 3.11.5–12**, with **Manu** and **Viṣṇu Purāṇa** as partial parallel ladders, and **Sūrya Siddhānta** for the prāṇa/nāḍī line.
